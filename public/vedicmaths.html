Vedic Maths

History

The origins of Vedic Maths can be traced back to ancient Indian scriptures known as the Vedas. These mathematical principles were rediscovered and popularized by Sri Bharati Krishna Tirthaji Maharaja in the early 20th century.

Introduction to Vedic Maths

Vedic Mathematics is a system of mathematical techniques that originated in ancient India. It involves a set of short-cuts and tricks to solve mathematical problems quickly and efficiently.

Vedic Mathematics is a system of mathematics that originated in ancient India and was rediscovered in the early 20th century. It offers a simplified approach to arithmetic and algebraic operations, making complex calculations easier and faster.

For example, one of the basic principles of Vedic Maths is the sutra (aphorism) "Ekadhikena Purvena," which translates to "By one more than the previous one." This principle simplifies multiplication and division by breaking down numbers into smaller, more manageable parts.

The Benefits of Vedic Maths

  • Speed and Accuracy: Vedic Maths techniques enable faster calculations with increased accuracy. For instance, using Vedic Maths, you can multiply large numbers mentally within seconds.
  • Improved Mental Agility: Practicing Vedic Maths exercises can enhance mental math skills and improve cognitive abilities. For example, techniques like "Vertically and Crosswise" require mental agility and concentration.
  • Applicability: Vedic Maths techniques can be applied to various mathematical operations, including arithmetic, algebra, geometry, and calculus. These techniques provide versatile tools for solving a wide range of mathematical problems.

Key Concepts in Vedic Maths

  1. Vertically and Crosswise: A technique for multiplying numbers. For instance, to multiply 23 by 12 using this technique, you multiply the vertical and crosswise elements to get the result (2*1 + 3*2 = 6 and 2*2 = 4), resulting in 276.
  2. By Addition and Subtraction: A method for quickly multiplying numbers by breaking them down into smaller parts. For example, to multiply 35 by 9, you can break 9 into 10-1 and add/subtract accordingly, resulting in 315.
  3. Digit Sums: Using the digital root of numbers to simplify calculations. For instance, to find the digital root of 345, you add the digits (3+4+5 = 12, and 1+2 = 3), resulting in 3.

Examples

1. Multiplication using Vertically and Crosswise Method

Question: Calculate 84 × 73 using Vedic Maths.

Solution:

            84
          × 73
          ------
            84
          + 84 × 7 (Crosswise)
          + 3 × 84 (Crosswise)
          ------
          = 84
          + 588 (Crosswise)
          + 252 (Crosswise)
          ------
          = 6132

So, 84 × 73 = 6132.

2. Square of a Two-Digit Number Ending in 5

Question: Find the square of 65 using Vedic Maths.

Solution:

Step 1: Multiply the tens digit by its successor (6 × 7) = 42

Step 2: Write down the square of the units digit (25).

Step 3: Combine the results: 6 (from step 1) | 25 (from step 2)

Step 4: Add a '0' at the end: 4225

So, the square of 65 = 4225.
